Below is a selection of advanced coursework in mathematics, economics, and statistics that has shaped my research training.

---

## Mathematics

**MTH 151 — Calculus I** &nbsp; 
<br>Foundations of differential and integral calculus including limits, derivatives, and integrals of single-variable functions.

**MTH 251 — Calculus II** &nbsp; 
<br>Techniques of integration, sequences and series, and an introduction to multivariable calculus.

**MTH 252 — Calculus III** &nbsp; 
<br>Multivariable calculus including partial derivatives, multiple integrals, vector calculus, and applications.

**MTH 222 — Introduction to Linear Algebra** &nbsp; 
<br>Systems of linear equations, matrix algebra, determinants, vector spaces, eigenvalues and eigenvectors.

**MTH 331 — Proof: Introduction to Higher Mathematics** &nbsp; 
<br>Transition to advanced mathematics with emphasis on writing and analyzing mathematical proofs. Topics include propositional and predicate logic, methods of proof, induction, sets, relations, and functions.

**MTH 432 — Optimization** &nbsp; 
<br>Optimization of functions of several variables, convexity and least squares, Kuhn-Tucker conditions, and linear programming.

**MTH 433 — Applied Linear Algebra** &nbsp; 
<br>Linear algebra with a focus on applications and computational implementation. Topics include singular value decomposition, matrix factorizations, stochastic matrices, PCA, image processing, and recommender systems.

## Economics

**ECO 405 — Economics of Strategy** &nbsp; 
<br>Microeconomic models applied to firm decision-making, integrating game theory and strategic reasoning to analyze competitive dynamics and managerial problems.

**ECO 414 — Mathematical Economics** &nbsp; 
<br>Mathematical techniques essential for economic theory and research, including calculus of several variables, linear algebra, classical and convex programming, comparative statics, and dynamic programming.

**ECO 420Y — R for Financial Economics** &nbsp; 
<br>Introduction to R for financial economics with emphasis on data analysis, simulation, and computational methods. Topics include data visualization, portfolio choice, mean-variance analysis, CAPM, factor models, and time-series methods.

**ECO 442 — International Monetary Relations** &nbsp; 
<br>Monetary aspects of international economic relations including exchange rates, balance of payments, and mechanisms of international monetary adjustment.

---

## Statistics

**STA 401 — Probability** &nbsp; 
<br>Development of probability theory with emphasis on statistical inference. Topics include Bayes' Theorem, distribution functions, expectation, moment generating functions, maximum likelihood estimation, and sampling distributions.
